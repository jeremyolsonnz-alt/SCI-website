Working Together

Work with Jeremy

The Guided Imagery Programme is free and self-directed, and it always will be. But some people want a guide beside them as they begin. If that's you, we can work together, one to one, from wherever you are.

"The practices are designed to be used alone. But the first weeks can be the hardest, and having someone to work through them with can be the thing that helps you keep going."

Everything on this website stays free. The audio, the scripts, the reflection journal, the reading behind it all, none of that changes. What I offer here is different: my time, one to one, for anyone who wants a more personal way in.

Jeremy, developer of the Guided Imagery Programme

What a session is

A session is a quiet, unhurried hour together over Zoom. We start where you are. Some people come wanting help establishing a rhythm with the practices. Some want the imagery adapted to their own life and their own body. Some simply want to be guided through a practice in real time, with a voice responding to them rather than a recording.

These sessions are personal support for living well after spinal cord injury. They are not a medical treatment, and they are not a promise of physical recovery, the same honest framing that runs through the whole programme. What they offer is company, experience, and a practice shaped around you.

What we might work on
  • Settling into a regular rhythm with the core practices
  • Adapting the imagery so it feels true to you, not borrowed
  • Working through the ordinary blocks: impatience, discomfort, the sense that nothing is happening
  • Learning to notice and use the daily rest windows the programme describes
  • Being guided through a full practice live, at your own pace
Before we meet (optional)

If you'd like, there's one small thing you can do beforehand. The site has a short self-reflection called Your Healing Style, eleven questions about how you tend to get through hard days. It takes about five minutes and nothing is recorded. Answering it in your own time, unhurried, tends to give a truer picture than anything we could piece together in the first few minutes of a call.

It isn't a test, and there are no right answers. Whatever comes back is a starting point we'll talk through together, not a label I'll fix you to. If you'd rather not, that's completely fine, we'll simply find your bearings in the session itself. If you do try it, you can use the copy button on the results and paste what you get into your email to me.

About Jeremy

I'm the developer of Living Beyond SCI, a free resource built around three pillars: quality-of-life research, hands-free harmonica, and the Guided Imagery Programme.

I hold a Master of Social Sciences majoring in Psychology from Waikato University, and a Diploma of Solution-Oriented Hypnosis, which I completed with Dr Robert McNeilly at Ampersand in Melbourne in 1998. My graduate research into quality of life after spinal cord injury was carried out under Professor Robert Cummins at Deakin University in Melbourne. The guided imagery approach is something I've been developing and refining for around thirty years.

Dr McNeilly is a medical doctor who studied directly with Milton Erickson, the physician widely regarded as the founder of modern clinical hypnosis. He brought the solution-oriented approach to Australia and taught it with a simple emphasis I've carried ever since: that the person already holds the resources they need, and the work is to help them find their own way to them. That's the spirit these sessions are offered in, gentle, respectful, and led by you rather than done to you.

I don't have a spinal cord injury myself. My work has always been alongside the people who do, as a researcher and a guide rather than someone handing down a treatment.
