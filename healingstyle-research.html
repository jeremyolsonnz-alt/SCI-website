Coping Strategies & Quality of Life

This self-reflection draws directly from graduate research conducted at Deakin University, Melbourne, which examined how 78 people with spinal cord injury across six countries coped with their injury and how satisfied they were with their lives.

It has two parts. The first asks about your coping strategies — the ways you manage things you can and cannot control — based on the Optimisation in Primary and Secondary Control framework (Heckhausen & Schulz, 1995) used in the original study. The second asks how important and how satisfied you are across eight areas of life, mirroring the Comprehensive Quality of Life Scale (ComQol; Cummins, 1997).

At the end, you receive a personal profile showing which coping strategies you rely on most, where the gaps between what matters and how things are going are largest, and how your results connect to the key finding from the research. Nothing is recorded. It takes about seven minutes.
